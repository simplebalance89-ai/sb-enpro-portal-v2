### Quick Material Compatibility Summary

**Polypropylene (PP):**
- Compatible: Water, dilute acids/bases, alcohols
- Not Compatible: Aromatic solvents, chlorinated solvents, oxidizers

**PTFE:**
- Compatible: Nearly all chemicals
- Not Compatible: Molten alkali metals, elemental fluorine

**Stainless Steel 316L:**
- Compatible: Most chemicals, high temp
- Not Compatible: Chlorides at high temp, HCl, hot concentrated H2SO4

**Buna-N (Nitrile):**
- Compatible: Petroleum oils, water, dilute acids
- Not Compatible: Ketones, esters, chlorinated solvents

**Viton (FKM):**
- Compatible: Oils, fuels, most chemicals
- Not Compatible: Ketones, esters, amines

### Key Technical Decision Criteria

**When to use Absolute vs. Nominal:**
- **Absolute (Beta 5000+):** When consistent, low contamination levels required; pharmaceutical, critical hydraulics, catalyst protection
- **Nominal (85-95%):** Adequate for coarse prefiltration only; not for final protection

**When to use Liquid/Gas vs. Liquid/Liquid Coalescers:**
- **L/G (SepraSol, Medallion):** Aerosol-sized liquid droplets in gas streams; <10um droplets
- **L/L (PhaseSep, AquaSep):** Stable emulsions in liquid/liquid systems; water-in-oil, oil-in-water

**Material Selection Decision Tree:**
1. **Temperature:** Determine max operating temperature
2. **Chemical compatibility:** Check compatibility charts
3. **Pressure:** Verify housing/seal material ratings
4. **Regulatory:** Food/pharma requires SS, sanitary, 3-A compliance
5. **Cost:** Balance performance vs. budget

### Testing Standards Referenced

**Filtration Performance Testing:**
- **Beta Ratio Testing (ISO 16889 / ISO 4572):** Multi-pass test method. Measures filter efficiency by counting particles upstream and downstream. Beta 5000 = 99.98% removal. This is THE standard for validating absolute-rated filters.
- **Bubble Point Test:** Integrity test for membrane filters. Measures the pressure required to force air through a wetted membrane. Non-destructive; used to verify filter integrity before and after use. Required for sterile filtration validation.
- **Challenge Testing (Bacterial):** Uses Brevundimonas diminuta (0.3um) at 10^7 CFU/cm2 to validate sterilizing-grade (0.2um) filters. Required for pharmaceutical applications.
- **DOP Test:** Dioctyl phthalate aerosol challenge at 0.3um; measures L/G coalescer efficiency. Result: 99.999% removal, 1 ppb downstream.
- **Modified ANSI/CAGI-400-1999:** Downstream aerosol concentration measurement for coalescers.

**Industry/Application Standards:**
- **Aqua-Glo:** Water content measurement in fuels (for L/L coalescer validation)
- **EPRI Guidelines:** Power plant startup chemistry hold points (iron, copper, silica, sodium limits)
- **3-A Standards (609-03):** Sanitary equipment for food/beverage/dairy/pharmaceutical (culinary steam)
- **NSF 61:** Materials in contact with potable water (REQUIRED for municipal)
- **FDA 21 CFR:** Food-contact materials compliance
- **USP Class VI:** Pharmaceutical-grade material biocompatibility
